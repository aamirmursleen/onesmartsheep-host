Best path: sell practical document outcomes, not "e-signature software". Pakistan buyers respond better to "send offer letter", "close proposal", "rental agreement", "parent consent", and "no printing/scanning".

Research snapshot

The market is ready enough for a paid test, but messaging must educate. Pakistan has a large digital audience, SMEs are paperwork-heavy, digital payments are now mainstream, and electronic signatures have legal recognition under Pakistan's Electronic Transactions Ordinance, 2002. The trust gap is the main friction, so ads should show proof, audit trail, mobile signing, and simple Pakistani use cases.

Digital reachDataReportal reports 66.9M adults reachable on social media in Pakistan in early 2025, with Facebook reach equal to 42.7% of local internet users.
SME baseSMEDA-linked reporting points to roughly 5.2M SMEs contributing around 40% of GDP and employing most non-agricultural workers.
Digital payment behaviorSBP FY25 reporting shows digital channels capturing a major share of retail transaction volume, making online workflows easier to sell.
Legal confidenceElectronic records and electronic signatures have legal recognition in Pakistan, with important exceptions for some document types.

Compliance and positioning rules

  • Do not claim MamaSign replaces every legal process. Some documents can have special legal requirements.
  • Do not advertise property sale transfer, wills, negotiable instruments, or government-certified digital signatures unless reviewed by counsel.
  • Say "recognized for many common business documents" instead of "valid for everything".
  • Show audit trail, signer email, timestamp, IP/device evidence, and signed PDF certificate in creatives.
  • Use Hinglish for Meta/TikTok and cleaner English for Google Search landing pages.
